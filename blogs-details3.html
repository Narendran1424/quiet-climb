Why Do Self-Promoters Get Promoted Faster Than Quiet Performers?

  1. Resources
  2. Insights
  3. Why Do Self-Promoters Get Promoted Faster Than Quiet Performers?

September 08, 2025

Full Article

  • It’s not because they’re more capable.
  • It’s because visibility is often mistaken for value.

Quiet professionals are told to “speak up more,” while leaders overlook the impact already being delivered.

Where The Bias Shows Up

  • Those who dominate airtime get recognition.
  • Ideas are only recognised when repeated louder by someone else
  • Leaders lean on quiet professionals behind the scenes, but overlook them publicly.

How Quiet Professionals Can Respond

When people feel they must act different to be accepted:

  • Share updates that connect your contribution to outcomes.
  • Use data, summaries, or short written notes to highlight impact.
  • Make your results visible, not just heard.

What Leaders Can Do

  • Track impact, not airtime.
  • Ask, “Who made this possible?” before giving credit.
  • Create recognition systems that reward substance, not noise.

The Quiet Climb Perspective

This isn’t about silencing self-promoters. It’s about building workplaces where every kind of talent is seen. Quiet professionals don’t lack ability. They need leaders and frameworks that help their Quiet Climb SignalTM cut through.
